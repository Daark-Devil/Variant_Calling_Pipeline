# Results Directory Guide

The `results/` directory contains all outputs generated throughout the somatic variant calling workflow, from raw mutation discovery to functional annotation and driver mutation prioritization.

```text
results/
├── 11871_somatic_PASS_variants.tsv
├── 11871_somatic_PASS_snpeff_table.tsv
├── 11871_HIGH_impact_variants.tsv
├── 11871_MODERATE_variants.tsv
├── 11871_cancer_gene_variants.tsv
├── 11871_driver_candidate_variants.tsv
├── 11871_driver_candidate_summary_table.tsv
├── 11871_variant_priority_summary.tsv
└── final_figures/
```

---

## 11871_somatic_PASS_variants.tsv

Contains all high-confidence somatic variants that passed Mutect2 filtering.

### Key Contents

* Chromosome position
* Reference allele
* Alternate allele
* Variant quality metrics
* Tumor evidence
* Normal evidence

### Purpose

Represents the primary set of somatic mutations detected in the tumor sample after removal of likely sequencing artifacts and false positives.

---

## 11871_somatic_PASS_snpeff_table.tsv

Contains functional annotations generated by SnpEff.

### Key Contents

* Gene name
* Variant effect
* Functional consequence
* Protein change
* Impact classification

### Purpose

Links each genomic variant to its potential biological effect.

Examples:

* Missense variants
* Nonsense variants
* Frameshift variants
* Splice-site variants

---

## 11871_HIGH_impact_variants.tsv

Contains variants predicted to have severe effects on gene function.

### Examples

* Stop-gain mutations
* Frameshift mutations
* Essential splice-site mutations

### Purpose

Identifies mutations most likely to disrupt protein function and contribute to disease.

### Biological Relevance

High-impact variants frequently contain candidate driver mutations and tumor suppressor alterations.

---

## 11871_MODERATE_variants.tsv

Contains variants predicted to alter protein sequence without completely disrupting gene function.

### Examples

* Missense mutations
* Protein-altering substitutions

### Purpose

Captures variants that may affect protein activity, signaling pathways, or cellular regulation.

### Biological Relevance

Many clinically important oncogenic mutations belong to this category.

---

## 11871_cancer_gene_variants.tsv

Contains variants occurring in known cancer-associated genes.

### Key Contents

* Cancer gene names
* Variant effects
* Functional annotations
* Mutation impacts

### Purpose

Focuses analysis on genes previously implicated in cancer biology.

### Biological Relevance

Reduces thousands of variants into a manageable set of biologically relevant candidates.

---

## 11871_driver_candidate_variants.tsv

Contains prioritized candidate driver mutations identified through impact filtering and cancer-gene annotation.

### Key Findings

Candidate driver genes identified include:

* PIK3CA
* CDH1
* ATM

### Representative Variants

* PIK3CA p.His1047Arg
* CDH1 p.Arg335*
* ATM p.Glu2052Lys

### Purpose

Provides the final list of mutations most likely to contribute to tumor development and progression.

---

## 11871_driver_candidate_summary_table.tsv

Contains a publication-style summary of prioritized mutations.

### Includes

* Gene
* Variant effect
* Impact category
* Protein change
* TLOD score

### Purpose

Provides a concise summary of the strongest candidate driver events detected in the tumor sample.

---

## 11871_variant_priority_summary.tsv

Contains cohort-level mutation summaries.

### Includes

* Total somatic variants
* HIGH impact variants
* MODERATE impact variants
* Cancer-gene variants
* Driver candidates

### Purpose

Provides a high-level overview of the mutation landscape.

### Major Findings

* 313 HIGH impact variants
* 2468 MODERATE impact variants
* 22 cancer-gene variants
* Multiple candidate driver mutations

---

## final_figures/

Contains publication-ready visualizations generated from the variant calling pipeline.

### Examples

* Driver mutation summaries
* Variant consequence distributions
* Mutation impact plots
* Cancer-gene prioritization figures

### Purpose

Provides visual interpretation of the tumor mutation landscape.
